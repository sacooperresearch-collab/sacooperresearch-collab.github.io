Independent research in cosmology focused on late-time empirical tests of foundational assumptions in the standard cosmological model. The work emphasizes model-independent analysis, direct observables, and a clear separation between measurement and inference based on early-Universe physics.

Publications

The Empirical Indeterminacy of the Sound Horizon
S. A. Cooper · Preprint (2025)

This work examines whether the sound horizon at recombination can be empirically constrained using late-time observations alone. Using cosmic chronometer measurements of H(z) together with the directly observed CMB acoustic angle, an empirical estimate of the sound horizon is obtained with substantially larger uncertainty than values inferred under ΛCDM assumptions. While consistent with the standard model, the results show that current late-time data do not fix the sound horizon with sufficient precision to function as an independent absolute scale, clarifying the role of assumed early-Universe physics in sound-horizon-based inferences.
